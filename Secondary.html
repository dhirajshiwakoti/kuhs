Nepali

Nepali is one of the key subjects in KUHS, being compulsory from class 1 to class 10. The Nepali department has set the following objectives to develop the standard of Nepali language, literature and communication skills:
  • To make the Nepali curriculum contextual
  • To transfer knowledge to learners bearing in mind the requirements for entering higher education
  • To enable learners to acquire grammatical skills through activity-based learning
  • To encourage vocabulary acquisition and creative writing development
  • To initiate discussion on cultural, economic, and social issues
  • To enrich the teaching of Nepali by keeping up to date with new methods


English

In the Nepalese context the commonly used evaluation method is taking paper and pencil examinations either four times or three times a year, and the final one has the highest weight. Students get 75% written and 25% listening and speaking test in Secondary English. KUHS adopts more student-centred approaches such as assessing marks from coursework, homework, organization, presentation, participation and overall effort. KUHS students read a variety of English literature from Shakespeare to post-modern writers to enrich their logic, creativity and skills.
By the end of Secondary School, KUHS students should:
  • Achieve higher grades in IELTS, TOEFL, A-Levels and other national and international examinations
  • Possess standard spoken English
  • Be able to produce sound discussion essays
  • Be able to use imaginative and innovative ideas
  • Believe in their thinking, reading, writing and speaking abilities
  • Have an appreciation for books, movies, arts and poetry
  • Possess a broad-based foundation for further study in language and literature


Science

Science classes play an important role in helping students acquire scientific knowledge, logical thinking, and analytical skills. KUHS uses the following methodologies to enhance learning:
  • Activities
  • Demonstrations
  • Group work
  • Peer work
  • Practical
  • Project work
  • Field visits
Students are assessed continuously through homework, classwork, projects, practicals, written work and presentations.

Mathematics

Mathematics helps students develop logical thinking, problem-solving skills, communication, generalizations, and creativity. To improve learning outcomes, KUHS implements the following strategies:

Learning Methodology

  • Students learn in pairs within groups and explain concepts to each other.
  • Each student solves problems based on worked-out examples and helps others as needed.
  • Partners are rotated to allow each student to be both learner and teacher.
  • Use of concrete (objects), semi-concrete (figures), and symbolic (notations) teaching materials.
  • Evaluation based on skills, understanding, and problem-solving.


Social Studies

Social Studies helps students understand human life, societal structures, institutions, culture, and global interrelations. The aim is to develop sincere, patriotic and socially responsible citizens. Teaching Methods:
  • Role play, community work, field-trips, hiking and observation
  • Group discussions and follow-up activities
  • Use of maps, globes, print and digital media
  • Teacher as facilitator to spark creative ideas
Evaluation:
  • Based on knowledge, understanding, practical and research skills
  • Focus on critical thinking and positive values
  • Preparation of in-house projects aligned with academic goals


Environment, Population and Health (EPH)

EPH education extends beyond schools to families and communities. Teaching is interactive and student-centered. Teaching Strategies:
  • Lectures, discussions, project works, and group activities
  • Trekking programs and practical tasks
  • Encouraging critical thinking through challenging topics
Evaluation:
  • 60% from daily class and homework performance
  • 40% from written exams
  • Students also evaluated on attitudes and engagement


Computing

As computers are an integral part of life and work, KUHS emphasizes the development of practical, adaptable computing skills. Key Learning Goals:
  • Effective use of PCs, Internet, and databases
  • Problem-based learning and project development
  • Adaptability to future technological changes
The curriculum is fluid and flexible to meet the demands of a dynamic digital world.
