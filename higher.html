Kathmandu University High School


Secondary School


+2 Science Programme

Affiliated to the National Examination Board (NEB)

Kathmandu University High School (KUHS) offers a rigorous and comprehensive +2 Science Programme affiliated with the National Examination Board (NEB). The curriculum is designed to provide in-depth subject knowledge and foster academic discipline among post-SEE level students, paving the way for higher education in fields such as medical science, engineering, management, and humanities.

The +2 programme is academically intensive, guided by updated syllabi aligned with international standards through the Curriculum Development Centre (CDC). The structure includes credit hours, theoretical instruction, practical demonstrations, examinations, and research-based activities to promote holistic development and future readiness.

Teaching Methodology

The faculty comprises experienced and dedicated professionals who ensure that each student receives quality instruction and academic support. Classes emphasize:
  • Theory lessons reinforced by practical demonstrations
  • Daily homework, individual and group assignments
  • Research-based projects and presentations
  • Personalized academic counseling
  • Continuous performance monitoring


Medium of Instruction

All courses are conducted in English. Students are expected to communicate in English during all school activities, both formal and informal.

Code of Conduct

Students are expected to maintain discipline, respect, and responsibility within the school community. The school enforces a clear code of conduct. Minor misconducts are addressed through counseling; repeated or serious violations can lead to expulsion.
Serious Offences Include:
  • Possession or use of tobacco, alcohol, or drugs
  • Inappropriate behavior or interaction
  • Leaving school or hostel without permission
  • Cheating during examinations
  • Verbal or physical abuse
  • Vandalism or damage to school property
  • Bringing unauthorized items (gadgets, jewelry, etc.)
  • Unauthorized driving of motorbikes/scooters

General Rules:
  • Timely attendance and classroom discipline
  • Timely submission of graded assignments
  • No junk food or eating in classrooms
  • Participation in all internal assessments
  • Silence and attentiveness in classrooms
  • Strict prohibition of political involvement and bullying


Uniform and Appearance

  • Students must wear appropriate, clean uniforms with proper shoes
  • Winter attire (muffler/cap) must be black
  • Boys must keep hair short and tidy; jewelry is not allowed
  • Girls must tie their hair neatly; makeup and long nails are not allowed


School Hours

Classes run from 7:30 AM to 2:30 PM, with breaks for snacks and lunch.

Extra-Curricular Activities

Students engage in 80 minutes of weekly sports and activities including basketball, football, table tennis, badminton, and more. Club activities enhance leadership and creativity.

Field Visits & Educational Tours

Students participate in subject-related field research and national/international educational tours, including exchange programs with partner schools in Australia.
